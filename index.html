Endora Marie McNeary

music / video / experiments

This project is a celebration of strangeness — of the beauty that lives inside a fragmented identity. My Spanglish Doors cover is about transforming what once felt disjointed, invisible, or out of place into something original and whole.

The Doors were one of the first bands my dad exposed me to, shaping my sense of sound and feeling, while my mother’s latinidad and the first-generation experience taught me depth, distance, and duality.

By blending languages, sounds, and memories, I reclaim my originality. I embrace the contradictions. I honor the wounds and the wonders.

About

My work lives where memory distorts into sound. Influenced by latinidad, psychedelia, and industrial noise, I build from fragments — rhythm, language, emotion — shaping worlds that feel intimate, uncanny, and alive.

I’m drawn to the spaces between genres: where distortion softens into melody, where ritual becomes rhythm, and where identity cracks open into something new.
